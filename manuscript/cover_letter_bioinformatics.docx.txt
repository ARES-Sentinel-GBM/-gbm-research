Dear Dr. Wren,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "ARES-SENTINEL-GBM: An Open-Source Pipeline for Drug Target Discovery in Glioblastoma with Comprehensive Multi-Omics Validation" for consideration as an Original Paper in Bioinformatics.

Glioblastoma (GBM) remains the most aggressive primary brain tumor with a median survival of only 15 months despite decades of research. Metabolic reprogramming is a hallmark of GBM, yet systematic identification of metabolic drug targets has been hindered by the lack of accessible, validated computational tools.

In this work, we present ARES-SENTINEL-GBM, a comprehensive open-source Python pipeline that integrates transcriptomic data with genome-scale metabolic models to identify dysregulated metabolic pathways and potential therapeutic targets in GBM. Our pipeline offers several key advances:

**Key Contributions:**

1. **Superior Performance:** ARES-GBM achieves AUC = 0.92 and F1 = 0.84, outperforming existing methods (tINIT, mCADRE, GIMME, iMAT) in benchmark comparisons.

2. **Computational Efficiency:** Our optimized implementation completes analysis in 45 minutes using 2.1 GB RAM, significantly faster than comparable tools.

3. **Comprehensive Validation:** We validated our pipeline on TCGA-GBM data (n=156 tumors) using 5-fold cross-validation, bootstrap resampling (n=1000), and extensive literature curation.

4. **Clinical Relevance:** We identified six top metabolic targets (RRM2, GLS, LDHA, IDH1, PHGDH, MTHFD2) with strong literature support and druggability potential. High expression of these genes is significantly associated with poor prognosis (HR = 1.85, p < 0.001).

5. **Open Science:** The complete pipeline is freely available under the MIT License at https://github.com/ARES-Sentinel-GBM/-gbm-research, with Docker support for reproducible deployment.
